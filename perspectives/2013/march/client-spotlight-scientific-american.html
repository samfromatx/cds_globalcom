iPad Customer Service and Beyond: CDS Global Helps Scientific American Launch New Ventures

Media companies everywhere are keenly aware of the iPad’s ever-growing presence in publishing. But while the iPad offers great potential, it also presents unique challenges. With more and more readers abandoning glossies for a glowing screen, publishers must not only offer tablet editions, but also provide quality customer service for these devices.

Scientific American offers an iPad edition as part of its premium subscription. More than just a digital version of the print issue, it provides readers with an entirely new, interactive reading experience. Scientific American knew it needed to provide iPad-specific customer service to ensure its valued customers maximize the tablet edition’s benefits and can use the device without difficulty.

With CDS Global already managing customer service for Scientific American’s approximately 400,000 print subscribers, expanding the partnership to include the iPad seemed natural. Scientific American visited CDS Global’s Des Moines location to conduct a full day of customer-service training.

The training sessions proved a positive experience for CDS Global and Scientific American alike. “From the beginning, it was clear that the people we were training were just as enthusiastic about the product as Scientific American and our customers. It was evident that CDS Global was really a good fit,” says Michael Thomas, senior digital product manager for Scientific American.

The CDS Global customer service representatives assigned to Scientific American now have expansive knowledge about how to use and troubleshoot the publication’s tablet edition for iPad, and receive regularly updated guides and additional assistance from Scientific American when needed. Samantha Watson, senior account manager for CDS Global, cites strong communication between CDS Global and Scientific American as the key to making iPad customer service a success: “There is an open line of communication where feedback from the customers reaches Scientific American quickly, and they respond and react to the needs of the customer to make the experience positive,” she says.

Scientific American will soon launch its own Web store platform, and plans to work alongside CDS Global in this endeavor. Christian Dorbandt, managing director of consumer marketing at Scientific American, says, “We believe in working with CDS Global to develop a small team of experts on Scientific American and the range of services and products that we offer – those are the flagship people of our brand.”

As Scientific American continues to expand its digital offerings, they rest assured CDS Global is providing quality customer service that allows them to focus on their goals. “We really think that our customers make all the difference. They are who we cater to, but our expertise is not customer support,” Thomas says. “Customer support is a real genuine business with a real set of skills you have to hire for, and CDS Global is a leader in that realm. When I come in to work, I’m not going to have an inbox full of angry customers because CDS Global takes care of our customers. As a product owner, that makes me feel really good. I know that as we move forward and have brand evangelists working as a dedicated team for us, I’m going to get a set of very confident, very enthusiastic people that are going to help us launch our Web store and help us grow into the future. We can’t do that without a very strong partner whose focus is customer service.”
