Intelligent Mail Barcode (IMb)

Also on Jan. 27, the USPS will no longer recognize the POSTNET barcode as a means for mailers to obtain automation discounts for letters, cards, flats and periodicals. Only an IMb will meet the requirements for automation postage. Either the basic or full-service IMb will satisfy this rule change. Qualified Business Reply Mail (QBRM) must contain an IMb to receive discounted postage. 

The use of IMb gives mailers the ability to receive additional postage discounts, obtain Address Change Service (ACS) at no charge and increase mail visibility to track the delivery of mail.

Failure to print an IMb after the effective date will cause an increase in postage of up to 23 percent as compared to current automation rates. QBRM postage will increase by as much as $.10 per piece without an IMb.

No Additional Entry Fee for Periodicals

Periodical mailers are no longer required to pay a fee for additional entry locations. Approved periodicals may now mail from any post office. Mailers can apply for free at a new post office for that office to be an additional entry location, whereas before mailers had to make that request at their original post office.

Change in Advertising Percentage

Periodical Requester publications will now be permitted to exceed the 75 percent advertising limitation on no more than 25 percent of the issues produced in any 12-month period. This change will allow publishers greater advertising flexibility during periods of high advertising volume.

New Option for Periodical Identification Statement

The USPS is offering publishers the option to direct Undeliverable As Addressed (UAA) mail to a Centralized Forwarding Site (CFS) rather than directly from the local delivery unit. The goals are to increase the number of electronic Address Correction System (ACS) notifications and lower the volume of hard-copy returns.

CURRENT
POSTMASTER: Send address changes to {Publication Title and Mailing Address}.

NEW
POSTMASTER: Send all UAA mail to CFS.
NON-POSTAL AND MILITARY FACILITIES: Send address corrections to {Publication Title and Mailing Address}.

CDS Global encourages the use of this new statement. Adoption should lower ACS costs and expedite the application of customer service transactions related to subscriber change of address information and Undeliverable As Addressed mail.
